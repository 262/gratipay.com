Want to get involved?

Thanks for wanting to help! This is the place to start. :-)

How does this work?

Gittip is a new kind of organization called an “open company.” Anyone can volunteer to help with almost anything at any time, and everyone who volunteers gets to take some of the money that has been given to Gittip—Gittip is funded on Gittip.

How is work divided?

We have three departments:

Business Development
Find people and teams that want to use Gittip for funding. Find companies and foundations that are interested in investing in people and teams via Gittip.
Community Engagement
Engage people on Twitter and Facebook and other social media, and manage our GitHub issue tracker.
Engineering
Build products for Gittip.

Where does work happen?

1. IRC (Internet Relay Chat)
We hang out in the #gittip channel on the Freenode network, and this is the best place to become a part of the community of people building Gittip. If you don't know what IRC is but feel brave, dip your toes in here: http://chat.gittip.com/.
2. GitHub
GitHub is different than Gittip! GitHub is where we manage our source code and keep track of and discuss open issues. Diving in here is the second-best way to get involved.
3. Twitter / Facebook
We try to listen for feedback and conversations about Gittip on Twitter, and we have the @Gittip Twitter handle and a Facebook page as well. Gittip's founder, Chad Whitacre, is highly active on Twitter as @whit537.
4. Google Hangouts On Air
We do video conferences on Google Hangouts, which we live-stream and post to YouTube.
5. Medium / Tinyletter
We have a “Building Gittip” collection on Medium where anyone can publish longer-form analysis and ideas. We also have an email newsletter called “Roll on, Gittip!” on Tinyletter, where we occassionally post summaries of work going on around the company. Here's the archive.
The primary audience for both of these is people building Gittip. If you want the inside scoop but don't need it in real time, subscribing on Tinyletter (here) or Medium (RSS) is a good option. For real-time, use IRC or Twitter (above).
6. Tumblr
We have a blog. The primary audience is the general public.
7. In Person
We're predominantly a distributed team, but we do sometimes come together at meetups and hackathons and conferences and whatnot.
8. Email / Text / Phone
We don't have private conversations if we can help it, but we'd rather hear from you privately than not at all. If you're not ready to jump in on one of the above forums, email Chad Whitacre or text/call him at +1-412-925-4220.

What next?

Tweet @Gittip indicating what department you are interested in, and one of our community liaisons will help you get started with your first project.
