Our Mission

Gittip’s mission is to jump start a gift economy. Tips are the foundation.

Imagine walking down the street and a bunch of your local townsfolk are lined up sitting there alongside a slew of celebrities and a random assortment of other humans. Each one is doing their own thing and has a jar out. You have a few bucks in your pocket and you’re in a good mood. Who would you tip?

There’s something called a genius grant that the MacArthur Foundation gives to about 30 people each year. It’s ten million dollars over five years, no strings attached. “Just keep being a genius. Please spend another five years making the world better for the rest of us.” The winners don’t even know they’re in the running until they get the phone call that they won.

Gittip is genius grants for the rest of us. Call them pretty-smart grants, or crowd-sourced genius grants.

Gittip is for the local coffee shop owner that’s really doing a lot for your town. You’re a fan. You buy coffee from her, but you want to do something more—a little something, not a big deal. If a hundred other people in your town feel the same way, it starts to add up. Now she can offer free coffee on Sunday mornings. Or whatever. She knows what she’s doing. No strings attached.

Gittip is for the musician who gives their music away for free. You love their work. You go to their shows, you actually pay for the downloads, but you want to make sure their family is covered. You want to chip in—a little something—so they’re freed up to make great music.

Gittip is for your favorite open source developer. You study their code, and you implicitly trust any software they write. You want to free them up to write even more of it.

Gittip is voluntary welfare for productive people.

Gittip is Kickstarter for people (not just projects).

Gittip is Flattr for people (not just content).

Gittip is a gift economy implemented atop the global exchange economy.

Details

Tips are weekly. One-off contributions aren’t enough. Expenses are ongoing. Income must be, too.

Tips are anonymous. The people you tip aren’t told who tipped them. Also, you can’t tip more than $1.28 a week. This way, tips come with no strings attached. Alice works on whatever she wants to work on. Bob tips whomever he wants to tip.

Tips are public. The amount you give is private, but the total amount you receive is public. Tip income closely reflects benefit to society, so calling out high earners encourages true value creation. Alice is concretely rewarded by a thousand Bobs for contributing to society.

Tips are distributed every Friday. Tips come out of your current balance (the money people have tipped you), and if that’s not enough, Gittip charges your credit card to make up the difference. We add an additional 10¢ + 3.9% fee when pulling money in from your credit card, to cover credit card processing (as well as hosting and other expenses). Recipients get the full face value of your tip to them. If you tip someone $1.28, they will have $1.28 in their Gittip account every Friday until you run out of money.

For example, let’s say that you have $5 in your account, and you are set up to tip $0.64 to 20 people. Here’s what we will charge your credit card on Friday:
