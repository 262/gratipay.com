Welcome to Logstown

by Robert Griffing

Depicted here is the arrival of French forces—priest included—at the village of Logstown in the run-up to the French and Indian War. This moment symbolizes the thick, intersecting tensions of geopolitics, commerce, religion, cross-cultural engagement, and face-to-face village life. Can we symbolically revisit this moment, and proceed differently?
